Who is she?

Based in New Jersey, Cosmic Cry encapsulates the creative output of self taught trans creative, Ashley Maximillian, who embraces an absurdist DIY attitude whose output consists of, and often combines, elements of digital and analogue visuals and sound. A through line of wild chaos magic and fearlessly pushing boundaries brings it all together, representing Ashley’s desire to channel her drive for exploration and transformation.

Ashley has been active as an artist since 2014 when one of her original songs was included on “Just Sign on the Dotted Line”, a compilation from Electro-Faustus, alongside legendary Japanese noise artist, Merzbow. After that, she contributed art and a few tracks to Uninvited Records. She is familiar with performance and pop up galleries, having collaborated with artist EKG and sound designer Jefferson Wells as The Avant Squad (TAS) from 2015-2017. Together, they transformed gallery spaces such as the Tender Trap in Greenpoint, Brooklyn and Works on Paper in Philadelphia into black voids of iconography, symbols, and noise. During that time, Ashley took part in a tour for Andrew Shirley’s film, “Wastedland 2,” playing shows up and down the East Coast. Since then, Ashley has worked toward building an online and in person community by performing and vending at local art events and even at the Trans Art Market in Philadelphia. She started mixing and mastering music in 2022, mostly working with antifascist black metal and punk bands. In 2023, one of her paintings was published in Variant Lit.

More recently, she began diving into a passion she had explored on and off over the years: programming. Ashley got her first taste of coding when she took an Intro to Java class in high school. Later explorations included customizing a weapon pack for DOOM II and learning about Processing, a programming language created specifically for artistic expression

She is driven by her love for all shades of teal and will always order the burger on the menu.

Apps

The following are examples of Ashley's coding output including a DooM II weapon mod, an experimental self portrait, and a bootcamp study guide, which is the only one that's been publicly deployed.

The DooM II "Spooky weapons" mod replaces every weapon in DooM II, as well as changing the sounds and mugshot of Doomguy to Doomgirl. Most of the replacements are simply aesthetic, the replacements for the chainsaw, rocket launcher, plasma rifle, and rpg act radically different from their vanilla counterparts.


The Chainsaw has been replaced with a flashlight.


The Rocket Launcher has been replaced with a Bio Blaster which fires balls of poisonous gas that explode after two bounces or colliding with an enemy. The explosion can harm the player if they are too close. The Bio Blaster's alt fire acts like a flamethrower, throwing a continous barrage of poisonous gas at the enemy while eating ammo at a quick rate.


The Plasma Rifle has been replaced with a Tesla Gun, inspired by the spread gun from the Contra games, fires multiple spiraling bolts of energy in a wide radius using 5 units of ammo with each shot.

Processing is a programming language focused on visual arts. Ashley's first project with the language was to make an abstract self portrait with simple shapes. Her secong project was a program that drew a line from point A to the mouse pointer when the left mouse button was pressed, she added functionality for a second point tied to the right mouse button and then combined it with the self portrait.

Before starting a coding bootcamp, Ashley's prework consisted of creating a study guide which suggested a topic to study in the inspace element of google chrome.
